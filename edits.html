The composition I was going for was more of a vintage, early 2000s New York feel.
The image is not fully in focus which added to the story in that it could have easily been taken on a disposable or early digital camera,
and as I kept editing, I was immediately reminded of my childhood. I could easily see the amber color time stamp in the bottom right corner, and it felt timeless.
The architecture and people of the city may change, but the culture never does. New York is the hustle and bustle capital of the world. The concrete jungle.

At first, I did my best to emphasize the pigments of the umbrellas but in doing so, highlighted the digital rainbow across the street,
and smaller colored parts of the trucks that were not as noticeable before. The photo again, is unfocused, but I think that adds to the allure of the bright colors,
transporting you from the corner of Hunter College at Lexington Avenue and straight to the state fair.
The Last Days of Summer.
